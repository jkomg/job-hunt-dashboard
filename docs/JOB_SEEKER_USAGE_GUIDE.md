# Job Seeker Usage Guide

This guide is for a job seeker using the hosted Job Hunt Dashboard for the first time.

The app works best when you treat it like a job-search command center:

- `Briefing` tells you what needs attention today
- `Check-in` captures your daily progress and sets tomorrow's priorities
- `Pipeline` tracks the jobs you are actively pursuing
- `Outreach` tracks the people connected to that search
- `Interviews`, `Events`, `Watchlist`, `Inbox`, and `Templates` support the rest of the process

If you are new, the easiest way to start is:

1. Set up your profile and make sure you can sign in cleanly.
2. Add your active jobs to `Pipeline`.
3. Add your networking contacts to `Outreach`.
4. Do a `Check-in` each day so `Briefing` becomes useful.

## First-Day Setup

On your first day in the app, keep it simple.

### 1. Open `Settings`

Use this area to make sure your account details look right.

- Confirm your display name and username
- Change your password if you were given a temporary one
- Add any optional links or setup details you want to keep handy

If your program includes staff support or admin support, this is also where some connected features may appear later.

### 2. Add the jobs you are already working on in `Pipeline`

Do not wait to build a perfect system first. Add the roles you are already applying to or following up on.

For each role, try to capture:

- company
- role title
- current stage
- date applied
- next action
- next action date or follow-up date
- job URL
- notes

That is enough to make the rest of the app useful.

### 3. Add the people tied to your search in `Outreach`

Think of `Outreach` as your networking tracker.

Add:

- recruiters
- hiring managers
- alumni
- former coworkers
- referral contacts
- anyone you want to follow up with later

For each person, try to record:

- name
- company
- how you know them
- outreach status
- follow-up date
- notes

### 4. Add upcoming conversations in `Interviews` and `Events`

If you already have interviews or networking events on the calendar, log them now so they do not stay stuck in your head.

## How To Use Each Section

## Briefing

`Briefing` is your home base. Open it first each day.

From a job seeker's perspective, this is the page that answers: "What should I work on right now?"

It usually surfaces:

- your top priorities
- follow-ups that are due
- upcoming interviews
- stalled roles
- recent activity
- messages that need attention

Use it to decide what matters today instead of scanning every part of the app manually.

Common-sense way to use it:

1. Look at the "needs attention" items first.
2. Handle anything time-sensitive, especially interviews and follow-ups.
3. Check your `Top 3` focus items.
4. Then move into `Pipeline` or `Outreach` to do the actual work.

If `Briefing` feels empty or generic, that usually means the rest of the app needs more dates, next actions, or daily check-ins.

## Check-In

`Check-in` is the habit-builder in the app.

It is not just a journal. It helps the dashboard understand your momentum and gives tomorrow's `Briefing` something useful to work with.

It tracks things like:

- mindset
- energy
- outreach sent
- responses received
- applications submitted
- conversations or calls
- a win from the day
- reflection
- tomorrow's top 3 priorities

Best way to use it:

- Fill it out once near the end of your workday
- Keep it honest, not polished
- Use the `Tomorrow's Top 3` field to set exactly three concrete next steps

Good `Top 3` examples:

- Follow up with recruiter at Acme about Customer Success Manager role
- Submit application for BrightPath implementation specialist role
- Send two alumni outreach messages for healthcare SaaS targets

Weak `Top 3` examples:

- Job search
- Networking
- Apply more

The more specific you are here, the more valuable your next day's `Briefing` becomes.

## Pipeline

`Pipeline` is the core of the app.

This is where you track every role you are actively considering, applying to, interviewing for, or closing out.

Use `Pipeline` to answer:

- What roles am I in process for?
- Which ones are moving?
- Which ones are stalled?
- What is my next action on each one?

As a first-time user, focus on keeping each role current rather than filling every field.

The most important fields are:

- company
- role
- stage
- priority
- next action
- next action date
- follow-up date
- date applied
- notes

A practical way to think about stages:

- early stages: interested, saved, researching
- middle stages: applied, recruiter screen, interview rounds
- late stages: final round, offer, closed

Common-sense workflow:

1. Add every real opportunity you care about.
2. Move the stage as soon as something changes.
3. Always leave a clear next action in the notes or next-action field.
4. Add dates so overdue follow-ups show up in `Briefing`.
5. Close out roles you are no longer pursuing so your board stays clean.

What makes `Pipeline` powerful is not just the status. It is the combination of:

- current stage
- who is involved
- what happens next
- when you need to act

If you only update one thing after each interaction, update the `next action` and the date.

## Outreach

`Outreach` is your relationship tracker.

Use it for the people around the job search, not the roles themselves.

That includes:

- networking contacts
- referral sources
- recruiters
- hiring managers
- former teammates
- event connections

This section is especially helpful if you tend to think, "I know I talked to someone at that company, but I cannot remember when."

A good contact entry usually includes:

- who they are
- where they work
- how warm the relationship is
- whether you already reached out
- when to follow up next
- what you talked about

Best practices:

- Add a note right after every real conversation
- Set a follow-up date before you leave the page
- Use status and warmth consistently so the list means something later

Example:

- Contact: Jordan Lee
- Company: Notion
- How we know each other: former coworker's introduction
- Status: reached out / replied / follow-up due
- Next action: send thank-you and ask one follow-up question

If a job in `Pipeline` is tied to a person, make sure both are updated. The role tells you what opportunity is moving; the contact tells you who can help move it.

## Interviews

Use `Interviews` for anything scheduled or completed that deserves preparation and follow-through.

This can include:

- recruiter screens
- hiring manager calls
- panel interviews
- mock interviews
- debrief conversations

Track:

- date and time
- company and role
- interviewer
- prep notes
- questions asked
- feedback
- next action

Practical routine:

1. Add the interview as soon as it is scheduled.
2. Store your prep notes there before the call.
3. Right after the interview, log what happened while it is fresh.
4. Add the thank-you or follow-up as the next action.

This keeps important details from disappearing between rounds.

## Events

`Events` is for job fairs, networking sessions, webinars, meetups, and similar events related to your search.

Use it when the event itself matters, especially if it may lead to outreach or interviews later.

Track:

- event name
- date
- type of event
- who you met
- follow-up tasks
- notes

A simple rule:

- if it is a conversation with one person, it may belong in `Outreach`
- if it is a scheduled group event or session, it belongs in `Events`

After each event, move the useful names into `Outreach` and set follow-up dates there.

## Watchlist

`Watchlist` is for companies you care about before there is an active application.

Use it for:

- dream companies
- companies that are not hiring yet
- places where you want to build a relationship first
- firms you want to check back on later

This is helpful when your search is targeted and you do not want interesting companies to disappear into bookmarks or browser tabs.

Keep it lightweight:

- company
- why it matters
- target role or team
- connection there
- date to revisit

When a watchlist company turns into a real opportunity, move that work into `Pipeline`.

## Inbox

`Inbox` is where you should look for messages or support threads tied to your account.

If you are part of a coaching program or staff-supported workflow, this may include:

- recommendations from staff
- follow-up questions
- updates on shared opportunities

From a first-time-user perspective, treat `Inbox` as the communication layer of the app.

Check it when:

- `Briefing` says messages need attention
- you are expecting guidance or recommendations
- you want to respond to staff support

If a message refers to a role or contact, update the relevant record after reading it so the app stays aligned.

## Templates

`Templates` is your library of reusable outreach messages.

Use it for messages you send often, such as:

- connection requests
- cold outreach
- referral asks
- thank-you notes
- informational interview requests
- follow-ups

The app supports placeholders like:

- `[Name]`
- `[Company]`
- `[Role]`

Good template habits:

- save a few proven messages you will actually reuse
- keep them short enough to customize
- do not over-automate your voice

A good template saves time without sounding generic.

## A Recommended Daily Rhythm

If you want a simple routine, use this:

### Morning

1. Open `Briefing`
2. Review due follow-ups, interviews, and stalled roles
3. Choose your top work from the existing priorities

### During the day

Work mainly from `Pipeline` and `Outreach`.

- update roles when stages change
- log contacts right after conversations
- add interviews and events as they get scheduled

### End of day

Open `Check-in`.

- record what you actually did
- capture one win
- reflect briefly
- set tomorrow's top 3

That one habit makes the app feel much more intentional the next morning.

## A Recommended Weekly Rhythm

Once a week, do a cleanup pass.

- archive or close out dead roles
- update stale follow-up dates
- review your watchlist
- make sure recent contacts have next steps
- check whether your pipeline still reflects reality

This prevents the app from turning into a graveyard of old applications.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Adding jobs without a next action
- Tracking roles in `Pipeline` but forgetting the people in `Outreach`
- Writing vague `Top 3` items in `Check-in`
- Leaving old roles open long after they are dead
- Saving lots of data once, then not updating it after real conversations

If the app ever starts to feel cluttered, the fix is usually not "use it less." The fix is to update fewer things, but update the important things consistently:

- stage
- next action
- next date
- notes

## What "Good" Looks Like

You are using the app well if:

- `Briefing` shows real priorities you recognize
- `Pipeline` tells you exactly where each role stands
- `Outreach` reminds you who needs a follow-up
- `Check-in` helps you start tomorrow without rethinking everything from scratch

That is the goal. The app should reduce mental load, not create more of it.
